Solid Waste Segregation Standards (India)

Quick guide for standard 4-way household sorting containers.

Dry Waste (Blue Bin)

Rinsed milk packets, clean plastic bottles, cardboard, paper sheets, metal tins, dry glass, and scrap wood.

Wet Waste (Green Bin)

Kitchen vegetable scraps, tea leaves, eggshells, pooja flowers, cooked leftovers, and garden leaf trimmings.

Sanitary Waste (Red Bin / Wrap)

Used diapers, sanitary pads, bandages, soiled medical masks, syringes, and clinical waste. Wrap in newspaper marked with a Red cross.

Domestic Hazardous & E-Waste (Orange/Black)

Discarded tube lights, battery cells, electronic wires, aerosol sprays, mosquito refill containers, and expired tablets.

Domestic Hazardous & E-Waste Rules

Never hand these over mixed with general dry or wet waste. They cause hazardous toxic leaks and worker injuries.

Batteries & Lead Accumulators

Risk: Causes garbage truck fires, leaks heavy acid toxins.

Solution: Store dry. Hand over separate to local electronic dealers or city e-waste center.

Mosquito Refills / Spray Cans

Risk: Vaporizer refills contain toxic chemicals (transfluthrin) and aerosol cans risk explosion.

Solution: Seal empty refill bottles in a bag. Send to HHW collection hubs.

Tube lights & CFL bulbs

Risk: CFLs contain toxic mercury vapor.

Solution: Wrap broken glass parts in cardboard; hand over to sanitation workers separately.

Expired Medicines & Drugs

Risk: Poisonous to environment and water supply when flushed.

Solution: Wrap inside paper marked with black tape, return to pharmacy take-back spots.
